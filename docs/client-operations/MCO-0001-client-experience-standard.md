# MCO-0001 — Client Experience Standard

**Version:** 1.0.0  
**Owner:** Director of Client Success  
**Status:** Active

## Purpose

Define a consistent, transparent and outcome-focused client experience from first contact through renewal.

## Principles

Professionalism, transparency, responsiveness, respect, accountability, technical excellence and continuous improvement.

## Client lifecycle

Lead → Qualification → Discovery → Proposal → Agreement → Onboarding → Delivery → Launch → Support → Growth → Renewal.

### Lead
Create the CRM record, capture the initial request, source and next action.

### Qualification
Evaluate business alignment, decision authority, urgency, budget, technical feasibility and organizational readiness. Decline poor-fit work respectfully.

### Discovery
Interview stakeholders, map processes and systems, identify pain points, establish constraints and define measurable success criteria. Produce a Discovery Summary.

### Proposal
Present the recommended outcome, scope, exclusions, assumptions, deliverables, schedule, pricing, acceptance process and next steps.

### Agreement
Complete the applicable MSA, SOW, payment terms, IP terms, privacy and security requirements, and contact assignments before implementation begins.

### Onboarding
Conduct kickoff, confirm communication channels, collect access, review risks, establish the project schedule and publish the onboarding checklist.

### Delivery
Provide regular progress updates, milestone reviews, decision logs, risk visibility and documented change control. Clients should not be surprised by preventable communication gaps.

### Launch
Coordinate deployment, acceptance, production verification, knowledge transfer and operational handoff. Produce a Launch Report.

### Support
Respond to incidents, defects and service requests according to contractual commitments. Track ownership, severity, response and resolution.

### Growth
Use business reviews to measure outcomes, reassess AI and automation maturity, and identify justified optimization opportunities.

### Renewal
Review delivered value, lessons, remaining needs, service adjustments and future plans before commitments expire.

## Communication standard

When an issue occurs:
1. Explain what happened.
2. Describe the known impact.
3. State what is being done now.
4. Present mitigation or recovery options.
5. Confirm the owner and next update point.

## Change management

Scope changes are documented, analyzed for timeline and cost, approved by authorized stakeholders and incorporated into the project baseline before implementation.

## Escalation

Defined escalation paths cover delivery risk, schedule impact, security events, contractual disputes and client dissatisfaction. Escalations prioritize safety, transparency and timely resolution.

## Client success metrics

- CSAT and NPS
- Milestone completion rate
- Budget adherence
- Response and resolution times
- Renewal and referral rates
- Adoption and measurable business outcomes

## Continuous improvement

Every engagement closes with an internal retrospective, client feedback, knowledge capture and proposed improvements to standards, templates, Framework components and delivery workflows.
