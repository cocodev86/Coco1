# MST-0001 — Five-Year Strategic Roadmap

**Version:** 1.0.0  
**Owner:** Executive Leadership  
**Status:** Active

## Vision

Build Metaphor into an AI-native software company that delivers consulting services, operates proprietary products and licenses the Metaphor platform to other organizations.

## Strategic pillars

### Services
Generate near-term cash flow through AI automation consulting, high-conversion digital experiences, workflow automation, internal tools and AI integration.

### Platform
Build reusable identity, knowledge, automation, AI, analytics, billing and delivery capabilities that reduce the cost and time of every future project.

### Products
Develop a focused portfolio of validated software products, including Metaphor OS, WYO, VerseLab and future vertical applications.

### AI
Make governed AI a first-class capability through agents, evaluations, prompt management, model routing and measurable automation.

### Brand
Establish authority through case studies, educational content, open-source work, technical writing and demonstrated client outcomes.

## Year 1 — Foundation

Primary objective: establish sustainable consulting operations and reusable delivery infrastructure.

Key initiatives:
- Launch and refine Metaphor consulting offers.
- Build the company website and lead-capture system.
- Deliver initial paid engagements.
- Establish the monorepo, design system and documentation library.
- Build Command Center v1 around actual operating needs.
- Introduce governed AI assistants for research, architecture, engineering and QA.

Exit criteria:
- Recurring or repeat clients
- Documented delivery workflow
- Positive project margins
- Reusable components in active use
- Reliable intake, proposal, onboarding and reporting processes

## Year 2 — Productization

Primary objective: convert proven internal capabilities into repeatable packages.

Key initiatives:
- Expand the Command Center.
- Launch the internal Developer Platform.
- Standardize automation packages and industry starter kits.
- Release the Client Portal.
- Mature AI orchestration and evaluation.

Exit criteria:
- Most new work starts from shared components.
- Delivery times decline without reducing quality.
- Internal platform adoption spans all engagements.

## Year 3 — Commercial Platform

Primary objective: pilot the external version of Metaphor OS.

Key initiatives:
- Production-grade multi-tenancy
- Workflow engine
- Plugin SDK
- Marketplace foundation
- Customer onboarding and billing
- Pilot customer program

Exit criteria:
- Paying pilot customers
- Stable platform APIs
- Documented implementation playbooks
- Measurable customer value

## Year 4 — Ecosystem

Primary objective: expand through integrations, partners and third-party extensions.

Key initiatives:
- Certified implementation partners
- Agent and workflow marketplace
- Broader integration catalog
- Public developer documentation
- Partner quality and governance program

Exit criteria:
- Active partner channel
- Third-party extensions
- Growing ecosystem-driven revenue

## Year 5 — Scale

Primary objective: operate Metaphor as a platform company with multiple durable revenue streams.

Key initiatives:
- Enterprise governance and compliance features
- Industry solution packs
- International readiness
- Advanced analytics and optimization
- Portfolio discipline and operational scale

Exit criteria:
- Services, platform and product revenue
- Mature operating controls
- Strong retention and referral economics
- Sustainable product portfolio

## Investment order

When resources are constrained:
1. Revenue-generating client work
2. Reusable platform capabilities required by real work
3. Internal productivity and reliability improvements
4. Validated proprietary products
5. Experimental initiatives

## Portfolio sequence

1. Metaphor Consulting
2. Metaphor OS for internal operations
3. Metaphor OS commercial pilots
4. WYO as a focused consumer product
5. VerseLab and additional products after the delivery engine can support them

## Annual planning cycle

Review outcomes, market conditions and portfolio performance; set annual themes; allocate capital and capacity; define quarterly objectives; review monthly; capture lessons; update the roadmap deliberately.

## Principal risks

- Attempting too many products simultaneously
- Weak cash flow or project margins
- Platform overengineering before demand
- Security, privacy or compliance gaps
- AI quality and cost volatility
- Founder bottlenecks
- Dependence on a single client, product or channel

Each major risk must have an owner, mitigation, trigger and review cadence.

## Executive scorecard

Track financial performance, qualified pipeline, client satisfaction, delivery predictability, gross margin, platform reuse, product adoption, engineering quality, AI effectiveness and team capacity.
